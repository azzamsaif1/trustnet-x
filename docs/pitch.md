# TrustNet X — Investor-Grade Pitch

## Slide 1: Problem

**AI agent marketplaces have a $2.1T blind spot: trust.**

When autonomous agents transact, the cheapest bid wins by default. But:
- A 0.20 SOL seller has a 40% no-show rate and 3 fraud signals
- No one prices the **risk** of hiring an unreliable agent
- Failed deliveries = lost funds, wasted compute, broken pipelines
- Honest, reliable agents are undercut by fraudulent low-ballers

**Result:** Race to the bottom. Market for lemons. Agent economies collapse before they begin.

---

## Slide 2: Solution

**TrustNet X is the underwriting layer for autonomous economies.**

Before funds lock in escrow, a consortium of underwriter agents:
1. Computes trust scores from 7 risk factors (reputation, fraud signals, delivery history, price deviation, quality, disputes, collusion)
2. Calculates **probability of failure** and **expected loss** per seller
3. Adds risk premiums that make unreliable sellers MORE expensive
4. Derives dynamic insurance fees and collateral requirements
5. Sets escrow policies with verification gates

**Real Cost = Bid + Risk Premium + Insurance Fee**

The cheapest bid is NOT the cheapest real cost. That's the insight. That's the product.

| Engine | Function |

**Explainability:** Every decision produces investor-grade reasoning — why cheap lost, why premium won, top risk factors, escrow rationale.

**7-Step Escrow:** WANT → BID → AWARD → DEPOSITED → DELIVERED → VERIFIED → RELEASED

**Solana Devnet Settlement:** Real on-chain proof with auto-fallback to simulation.
